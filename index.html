How to run it.

Stats.

Timeframe

60–120 minutes

Group Size

3-5 teammates of mixed disciplines

Facilitation level

Requires dedicated facilitator

Materials needed

Cards, Sharpies, Wall

Steps.

1.

Firstly you’ll need to assemble the team and a facillitator for the session. It’s good to have the individual and their manager or lead decide the right mix together.

2.

Now set the time and place and send the introduction email so people have context and an opportunity to think about what feedback they might share.

3.

On the day of, have the session set-up with the printed worksheets or Trello board. Open the session by explainig the ground rules.

4.

Now it’s time to get into sharing feedback. Allow the team five minutes to individually card up feedback for the first worksheet - Brightspots. Then take it in turns to share your cards.

5.

Repeat this process for all four worksheets.

Why this works.

Why good feedback takes a team

The Personal Retro is based on two ideas - trust can be acquired by revealing information about you to others and learning about yourself from others helps you make sense of your unique value to the team.

Why feedback is hard

As humans we have a strong desire to grow but also need to have a strong sense of identity. This fundamental conflict makes giving & receiving feedback difficult. The Personal Retro provides a framework for people to share feedback easily and constructively.

How the personal retro combats imposter syndrome

Replace self-doubt with shared understanding. Recognise your own growth and progress by getting perspectives that only your team members can provide so that you can combat the self-doubt that causes imposter syndrome.

Common questions.

The most effective Personal Retros come from involving the right team to deliver feedback that’s honest and with the positive intent to help the individual grow.

The team should represent a diverse mix of people – across leads, team members and peers – and probably shouldn’t be larger than six people total (including yourself).

In addition to finding the right team, we recommend setting the context and ground rules for each team member who is involved a few days before the Personal Retro so that they can prepare to give considered feedback

The Personal Retro is designed to help you and your team deliver feedback in a purposeful and productive way. While sharing your feedback face-to-face can feel strange at first, the prompts for each section are meant to guide you and ensure that the feedback is phrased appropriately.

During the session, the facilitator or individual can also make the call to pause for 5 minutes, or to conclude the session if positive outcomes aren’t being achieved.

Personal Retros can be really effective in periodically reflecting on growth. We have found that a personal retro every 3-6 months provides the individual enough time and experience to draw feedback from.

    In our experience, Personal Retros work best within teams and environments that:
  • Regularly conduct project or team retrospectives (or similar team learning rituals)
  • Are comfortable providing and receiving peer-to-peer feedback
  • Worked together for at least 1 project (ideally more)
  • Have a sense of trust and support among individuals
      • What to avoid:
  • Giving feedback on event, behaviour, or observation that happened over 6 months ago – it is important that the feedback is timely and relevant to the individual
      • Personal Retros should not be used to:
  • Replace 1-1 feedback conversations
  • Share feedback that is better shared in confidence with the individual
  • “Get something off your chest”
